🎯 The Core Concept

The Big Idea: Use an aggressive profit-making system (ATTACK), but when it loses, switch to a conservative high-win-rate system (RECOVER) to erase the loss. Then go back to attacking.

⚔️ The Two Modes Explained

⚔️ ATTACK MODE (S2 System)

Aggressive profit generation using statistical edge

  • When: Wait 5 minutes into 15-min window
  • Entry: When edge ≥ 10% (history vs Kalshi price)
  • Win Rate: ~60%
  • Bet Size: 2-8% of bankroll (scales with edge)
  • Purpose: Compound profits aggressively
Edge Calculation
edge = historical_persistence - kalshi_implied_odds

Example: At minute 5, history says 71% persist.
Kalshi prices at 55c (55% implied).
Edge = 71% - 55% = 16% ✓ (≥10%, take trade)
Dynamic Bet Sizing
scale = (edge - 10%) / 20%
bet% = 2% + (scale × 6%)

• 10% edge → 2% of bankroll
• 20% edge → 5% of bankroll
• 30% edge → 8% of bankroll

🛡️ RECOVER MODE (S3 System)

Conservative loss recovery using high win-rate

  • When: Wait 10 minutes into 15-min window
  • Entry: Only at 80-92c prices (sweet spot)
  • Win Rate: ~90%
  • Bet Size: Calculated to recover loss + 10%
  • Purpose: Erase ATTACK losses safely
Why 80-92c Works
At minute 10, persistence is 84%+
Prices 80-92c = slight positive edge
Combined = ~90% actual win rate
Recovery Bet Sizing
target = loss × 1.10 (recover + 10% buffer)
profit_per = (100¢ - entry) - fee
contracts = target / profit_per

Example: Lost $50, entry 85c
Target = $55, profit = 14¢/contract
Contracts = $55 / $0.14 = 393

🧠 Why This Strategy "Always Wins"

✅ When Recovery Works (91% of time)

1. ATTACK loses $50
2. RECOVER bets to win back $55
3. RECOVER wins (90% win rate)
4. Loss erased, back to ATTACK
5. Continue compounding profits
❌ When Recovery Fails (9% of time)

1. ATTACK loses $50
2. RECOVER attempts but loses too
3. Can't afford bigger recovery bet
4. Just go back to ATTACK anyway
5. ATTACK is profitable, climb back up!
There's no real failure mode!

Either recovery works and erases the loss,
OR you just keep attacking (which is profitable anyway).

The worst case is literally "keep doing the profitable thing"
